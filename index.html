About Me

I am Ayinla Mubarak, a Ph.D. candidate in Organic Chemistry at the University of Massachusetts Lowell. My research intersects organic chemistry, dynamic covalent chemistry, and material science. I focus on the synthesis of complex materials via nitroaldol and other aldol-type reactions, targeting applications in gas storage and environmental remediation.

Research

My work investigates the thermal model of selective laser melting (SLM) for multimaterial systems, examining polymer behaviors under varying laser parameters. Another focus is on capillary-driven debinding of liquid paraffin wax from alumina ceramic structures under isothermal conditions.
